About the METROPOLENS

METROPOLENS is a photography contest open to all students enrolled in State Universities and Colleges in Region VI. Participants will capture images that showcase the relationship between technology and urban environments through three distinct themes: Technology in Urban Setting, Architectural Innovation, and Impact on Daily Life.

This competition provides a platform for student photographers to document and interpret how technology shapes our modern cities and urban experiences. Through their creative lens, participants will explore and showcase the evolving technological landscape of urban spaces in Region VI.

Eligibility

Qualifications

The contest is open to all students enrolled in all State Universities and Colleges (SUC’s) in Region VI who are interested in showcasing their photography skills.

LINK.exe members and officers are NOT eligible to participate.

All interested participants must register and submit their entries through the provided Google Forms.

Participants must provide a valid student ID and proof of enrollment upon submission.

GUIDELINES

  • Photos must be taken within Region VI only.
  • Entry Composition: - Each participant must submit three (3) original photographs, one for each of the following themes: Technology in Urban Setting – Capture how modern technology integrates into city life. Architectural Innovation – Emphasize the role of technology in shaping urban infrastructure. Impact on Daily Life – Showcase how technology influences people’s daily routines in the city.
  • Photography Device -Only photos captured using a digital camera (DSLR, mirrorless, or point-and-shoot) are allowed. Photos taken using mobile phones, tablets, action cameras (e.g., GoPro), or drones are strictly prohibited.

TECHNICAL SPECIFICATIONS

Images must be submitted in JPEG/JPG format, with a minimum resolution of 300 dpi.

Image size should be between 3MB to 10MB.

Landscape orientation is highly encouraged for uniformity in exhibit layouts, but portrait orientation may be used for detailed and creative shots.

Raw files may be requested for verification if needed.

EDITING AND ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS

Minor post-processing is allowed, limited only to:

  • Adjustments in brightness, contrast, exposure, white balance, cropping, sharpness, and color correction.
  • Black-and-white conversion is acceptable but must maintain clarity of subject matter.
  • Not allowed:
  • Composite photography, digital manipulation (e.g., adding/removing elements), HDR merging, or stitched panoramas.
  • Use of filters from mobile apps (e.g., Instagram, Snapseed, Lightroom mobile presets).
  • Any form of AI-generated content or AI-assisted image editing.
  • Watermarks, frames, texts, or signatures on the image.

ORIGINALITY & INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Ownership and Rights

  • All submitted photographs must be original works taken by the participant and must not have been previously published, exhibited, or submitted to other competitions.
  • By submitting an entry, the participant certifies that: They are the sole owner of the photo. They have obtained necessary permissions from any individuals featured in the photo.
  • The organizers reserve the right to use submitted photos for non-commercial purposes, such as educational exhibits, publicity materials, and documentation, with proper credit to the photographer.

SUBMISSION

Submission Details

  • Deadline of Submission: MAY 1, 2025 at exactly 11:59 PM. Late submission will not be entertained.
  • Participants must upload their entries to their own Google Drive and paste the link into the provided Google Forms.
  • Participants must compile their three (3) photos in a Google Document, with each photo followed by its corresponding caption placed directly below it.
  • Once completed, the Google Document must be downloaded as a PDF file and uploaded via the provided Google Form submission link.
  • In addition to the PDF, participants must also upload the original JPEG/JPG files of the three (3) photos in the same Google Form.
