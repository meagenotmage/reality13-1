About RealITy

What is RealITy

RealITy Festival or RealITy is an annual tech rally by LINK.exe under the Pillar of Program. It features speakers from the tech industry and showcases cutting-edge innovations. The event connects technology trends with society, giving students and professionals a space to explore, learn, and get inspired by the latest in tech.

RealITy XIII: Metropolis

Now in its 13th year, RealITy focuses on smart cities with the theme METROPOLIS, inspired by Iloilo City’s digital transformation. The event empowers youth through expert talks and innovation-driven contests, giving students a chance to explore tech trends and showcase their own smart city ideas.

Objectives

RealITy XIII promotes learning and discovery in smart city tech, encourages ethical discussions on data use, empowers participants through training and resources, and inspires creativity through collaborative, innovative platforms.

Featured Speaker

Learn from the industry expert

Engr. Laurelly Joyce 'LJ' Aporto

Remote Sensing Specialist

Geodetic Engineer

Engr. Laurellly Joyce 'LJ' Aporto, a Senior Product Design Specialist for the Smart METRO Project. A proud alumna of the University of the Philippines with a degree in Geodetic Engineering, Engr. LJ is a passionate researcher and seasoned geospatial scientist. She’s known for transforming complex spatial data into meaningful insights that drive real-world impact—particularly in sustainable urban planning, disaster resilience, and environmental conservation. Her work on the Smart METRO Project is helping shape the Metro Iloilo–Guimaras area into a cutting-edge smart metropolis.

Keynote: "Building the Cities of Tomorrow"

Engr. LJ explores how emerging technologies are reshaping urban spaces into efficient, sustainable, and people-centered environments. From GIS to remote sensing, she’ll share insights on how smart city implementations are transforming local governance and community planning. Attendees can expect a compelling discussion on the future of digital urban planning, data-driven decision-making, and what it truly takes to design cities that work for everyone.
