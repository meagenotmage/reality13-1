IDEATHON

QUALIFICATIONS

The contest is open to all tech enthusiasts and students in all satellite campuses of West Visayas State University.

Each team must have a minimum of three (3) and a maximum of five (5) members per team.

LINK/Inc Members and Officers are NOT eligible to join the contest.

All participants must register using the official form. Deadline for submission is on the pre-screening phase.

Each team must register with finalized members. Changes are not allowed after submission.

Walk-in registrations during the event are NOT allowed.

PITCH TOPICS

Each team must pick at least one of the themes for their pitch, ensuring it aligns with at least one Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) and demonstrates clear social, environmental, or economic impact:

A) Smart Solutions for Resilient Cities

Think of one urban problem and design a simple tech-based solution, like a basic smart sensor system, to improve it.

B) Agri-tech: Learning Agriculture the Smart Way

Create a solution that enables building sustainable farming techniques to improve crop yields and train new farmers.

C) One Tap Health

Design a solution that simplifies health info, tracks medications, or connects users to care providers.

D) Event Reservation & Ticketing App

A customizable online Box Office app for citizens to reserve spaces or tickets for public events.

COMPETITION PHASES

ELIMINATION ROUND

Submission Requirements

  • Participants must submit a 3-5 minute video explaining their product or idea.
  • Final Pitch Deck (PDF or PPT format) – A complete pitch deck to be used in the Final Round if your team advances.

Video Guidelines

  • Must clearly explain the idea covering:
    1. Problem Statement – What issue does your idea address?
    2. Solution Description – How does your product solve the problem?
    3. Market & Interest – Who are the potential users of your solution?
    4. Team Composition – Introduction of team members and their roles.
  • The video should not be more than 5 minutes long and should utilize the natural voices of the presenters. No text-to-speech voice enhancements or AI-generated voice overs are allowed.

Pitch Deck Guidelines

  • Maximum of 12 slides (including title and closing).

Submission Format and Deadline

  • Video file (MP4 or MOV) or provide a YouTube/Google Drive link
  • Pitch Deck format: PDF or PPT
  • All submissions must be submitted via email along with the team's Google Drive folder to link@wvsu.edu.ph on or before May 1, 2025, 11:59 PM.

FINAL ROUND

Presentation Format

  • The same pitch deck submitted in the Elimination Round must be used. Pitches should include:
    1. Introduction & Problem Statement
    2. Solution
    3. Prototype or Demo (if available)
    4. Business Model & Market Strategy
    5. Scalability & Future Roadmap
  • Each team has up to 10 minutes to present their pitch.
  • The timer will begin as soon as the presenter speaks their first word. When the allotted time is up, a ring sound will signal the end. At that point, the sound technician will turn off the presenter's microphone.
  • After the presentation, each team will be given a maximum of 10 MINUTES to answer the questions from the judges. The given time is subject to the judge's contentions.

Presentation Rules

  • Before the competition begins, each group will draw their group number to determine the order of pitching.
  • Each team must select only TWO REPRESENTATIVES to present their pitch presentation. However, during the Q&A session, other team members may participate in answering questions.
  • The representatives and the team are preferred to be in semi-formal attire or business attire.

GENERAL RULES AND POLICIES

Disqualification Grounds

  • Submission of plagiarized content or failure to meet originality and authenticity standards.
  • Violation of confidentiality or intellectual property policies, including unauthorized use or disclosure of another team's ideas.
  • Non-compliance with eligibility criteria or failure to adhere to technical submission requirements.
  • Failure to confirm attendance as a finalist within three (3) days of notification, leading to automatic forfeiture of the spot.
  • Unprofessional behavior or misconduct toward organizers, judges, staff, or fellow participants, including harassment or misconduct.

Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

  • All content submitted during both the elimination rounds and final rounds shall remain the exclusive intellectual property of the respective teams.
  • Participants are strictly prohibited from sharing, reproducing, or plagiarizing pitch ideas outside the scope of the competition without prior written consent from the original team.
  • By participating in the event, teams grant the organizers the right to use their concepts for promotional purposes only. The organizers ensure that all materials and ideas will remain confidential and will not be accessed or utilized for any other purpose.
  • Any breach of this confidentiality clause will be taken seriously and may result in disqualification from the competition.

Professionalism and Conduct

  • All participants are expected to conduct themselves professionally and respectfully toward judges, organizers, and other teams throughout the competition.
  • Judges' decisions are final and must be respected by all participants.

Amendments & Rule Changes

  • The organizers reserve the right to update the rules and policies as necessary. Any changes will be communicated to participants in a timely manner.
